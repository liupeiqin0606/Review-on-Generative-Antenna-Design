This repository serves as a companion to the review paper **"Deep Learning-Enabled Generative Antenna Design: Review and Prospects,"** published in *IEEE Transactions on Antennas and Propagation*. This work explores the paradigm shift from traditional optimization to **Generative Antenna Design (GAD)** powered by neural network (NN)-based deep learning (DL).

## 📖 Overview
The rapid growth of massive MIMO and intelligent surfaces has pushed traditional antenna design methods to their limits. This paper provides a comprehensive review of the historical development and diverse applications of NN-based antenna optimization, covering individual elements, multi-antenna systems, and periodic structures.

### Key Contributions
* **Comprehensive Review**: Traces the evolution of antenna design from simple geometry optimization to complex generative synthesis.
* **Algorithmic Deep-Dive**: Discusses the implementation and potential of advanced architectures, including:
    * **Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs)** and **Conditional GANs (cGANs)**.
    * **Variational Autoencoders (VAEs)**.
* **Physics-AI Integration**: Highlights the critical role of **Prior Knowledge (PK)** in guiding DL models to ensure physical consistency and data efficiency in electromagnetic synthesis.
* **Performance Frontiers**: Demonstrates how DL-enabled generative methods can break through the performance ceilings of conventional designs to discover new antenna functionalities.

---

## 🛠️ Concepts & Methodologies

The paper categorizes the landscape of DL-enabled design into several core areas:
1. **Optimization vs. Synthesis**: Contrasting conventional scale optimization with pixelated geometry synthesis for increased degrees of freedom.
2. **Periodic Structures**: Reviewing the role of GANs in the design of metasurfaces, metalenses, and frequency-selective surfaces.
3. **Multi-Antenna Systems**: Exploring how deep learning addresses mutual coupling and beamforming challenges in large-scale arrays.
4. **Knowledge Discovery**: Discussing how generative models can assist researchers in uncovering new electromagnetic patterns and principles.

---

## 🚀 Future Prospects
The review concludes by outlining the roadmap for the future of GAD, focusing on:
* **Physics-Informed Neural Networks (PINNs)** for real-time electromagnetic solvers.
* **Small-sample learning** to reduce the cost of full-wave simulation data generation.
* **Automated discovery** of intelligent electromagnetic skins and surfaces for 6G environments.
